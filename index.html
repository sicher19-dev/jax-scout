The Read

They've already seen the plan. Change the sequence.

Round 1 was a shutout — but 57% of what he threw KC was changeups and sweepers, and he's facing the same nine just six days later. They'll sit soft.

The fix isn't a new plan, it's order of operations: establish the fastballs early this time. Get ahead with the sinker, put a four-seam up — especially to the contact lefties who already barreled the cutter and soft stuff. Then the put-aways still play.

The reads that worked hold: cambio to Caglianone, sweeper to Perez, soft to Misner, no changeup to the changeup-mashers. Just don't run them in the same sequence twice. And there's one bat with no tape on any of it — Witt's back.

SPEAK GRIFFIN · 30 SEC

Round 2 with KC, six days later, in their yard. Last time you shut them out — 5 and 0, seven punchouts — and the plan worked. But you threw them changeups and sweepers more than half the night, and now they've got fresh tape. So the one adjustment: show them the fastball early this time. Get ahead with the sinker, put a four-seam up.

Massey is the tell — he squared you up because he saw exactly one heater all night. Climb the ladder on him. Everything else holds: Caglianone is changeup-only (you K'd him twice on it), Perez can't touch the sweeper-or-cambio (a K and a double play), Misner and the soft lefties chase the changeup down.

The new face is Witt — back in the lineup, hitting second, and the one bat that hasn't seen your plan. Pitch him careful: spin down, and never the changeup. Kauffman's a big park — let the fly balls die out there.

One more from the model: your flat-swing top of the order — Jensen, Witt, Collins — covers the soft stuff, so don't get cute up there; pound the zone and let them put it in play. The bats that can't cover your sweep-and-dive sit in the middle — Perez, Rojas, Misner. That's where the changeup and sweeper bury people.

Expect about 5 and a run or two. The floor is the lefties squaring the cutter early if you fall behind in counts — so get ahead, and make them beat the fastball.
